## Segment 1 · Hook (0:00 – 0:15)

> Every cyberattack starts the same way — not with hacking, but with research. Attackers spend hours mapping your company through LinkedIn, GitHub, Shodan, and breach databases — all before they ever touch a system.

## Segment 2 · Brand Intro (0:15 – 0:32)

> Meet MirrorTrap — the platform that lets you see yourself exactly the way an attacker does. Tagline: see yourself through a hacker's eyes. Today I'll walk you through the entire experience in under four minutes.

## Segment 3 · Landing & Dashboard (0:32 – 0:58)

> This is the MirrorTrap landing page — a deep black canvas with a live attack-graph particle field. One click on Try Hacker's Eye View takes you straight into the platform. The Dashboard shows your live Attack Readiness Score, recent scans, active decoys, and threat alerts updating in real time.

## Segment 4 · Company Domain Scan (0:58 – 1:35)

> Let's run a real scan. We type a company domain — the toggle above defaults to Company Domain. The animated terminal streams five live OSINT sources at once: HaveIBeenPwned, Shodan InternetDB, crt dot S H subdomains, GitHub public search, DNS records, and Security Headers. Within seconds you get an Attack Readiness Score, severity-ranked findings, an estimated time to exploit, and an estimated breach cost in rupees.

## Segment 5 · Personal Email Scan (1:35 – 2:15)

> Now the new module — Personal Email. Click the Personal Email pill, drop in your Gmail. The terminal reels through HaveIBeenPwned, paste sites, dark-web indexes, data brokers, and account-takeover scoring. Out comes a breach summary card, per-breach details with the exact data classes exposed, an attacker-capability narrative explaining how each leak gets weaponised, and a six-step personal protection checklist with a live progress bar.

## Segment 6 · Hacker's Eye View — Kill Chain (2:15 – 2:45)

> Switch over to the Hacker's Eye View page. The AI Attack Path Engine takes every real finding and stitches them into a five-step MITRE ATT&CK kill chain — Reconnaissance, Initial Access, Execution, Lateral Movement, Exfiltration. Toggle any node between attacker view and defender fix to see exactly how to stop it.

## Segment 7 · Breach Simulator (2:45 – 3:10)

> Here's the demo showstopper — the Simulate Breach button. One click plays out a full-screen, twenty-second compressed replay of your domain getting breached: matrix rain, live timer, files exfiltrated, bytes stolen, and an auto-generated ransom note. The end card reads — this breach took four minutes seventeen seconds. MirrorTrap catches it in three hundred milliseconds.

## Segment 8 · Dark Web Mirror, Hackability & Dossier (3:10 – 3:35)

> Right below it, the Dark Web Mirror renders your org as a marketplace listing — seller reputation, price in Bitcoin, last-updated timestamp, proof-sample terminal. The Hackability Score gives a single zero-to-hundred number with a five-category breakdown, and one click downloads a printable Attacker Dossier — formatted exactly like a real threat-intel report.

## Segment 9 · PhantomShield Decoys (3:35 – 3:55)

> PhantomShield is our offensive layer. Deploy fake credentials, fake admin portals, fake API keys into the same public channels attackers scrape. The moment anyone touches one, MirrorTrap captures the IP, geolocation, tools, and full attack path — zero false positives. If it fires, someone real touched it.

## Segment 10 · Financial Impact, Attack Origin & AI Chat (3:55 – 4:20)

> Reports gives you a Financial Impact Analyzer — what's at risk, MirrorTrap's protection value, and industry benchmarks from IBM and Verizon DBIR, with a one-click downloadable risk report. Alerts shows live Attack Origin Intelligence — a country bar chart, attack-type donut, and the hottest IPs hitting you right now. And on every page, the floating AI chat assistant answers questions about your score, decoys, and alerts.

## Segment 11 · Closing Pitch (4:20 – 4:42)

> Thinkst Canary charges seventy-five hundred dollars a year and does half of this. MirrorTrap does it for nine-ninety-nine rupees a month. Average breach cost — four point nine million dollars. MirrorTrap Enterprise — one point two lakh a year. That's not a product. That's insurance. MirrorTrap. We don't wait for attackers to knock. We already know they're outside.
